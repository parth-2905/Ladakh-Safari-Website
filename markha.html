Markha Valley Trek

A 5-day adventure through the stunning Markha Valley in Ladakh, India

Scroll Down ↓

Day 1: Leh to Sara

Morning drive to Sara and camp overnight. This is your first campsite where you'll spend the rest of the day acclimatizing with light walks around the area.

Altitude 3619m
Activity Drive & Camp

Day 2: Sara to Markha

Today is a pleasant hike up the Markha valley. Visit the old monastery and the ruined fort. Trek along the scenic Markha River, crossing it several times over makeshift wooden bridges.

Altitude 3760m
Distance 8 km
Duration 4 hours

Day 3: Markha to Hankar

Walk along the river and visit Techa gompa which is built on a cliff near Umlung village. After leaving Umlung, you'll catch your first view of Kangyatse Peak (6,400m).

Altitude 4030m
Distance 11 km
Duration 5 hours

Day 4: Hankar to Nimaling

The path from Hankar leads you to Nimaling, which is the summer grazing ground for the cattle. This is the highest campsite of the trek, offering stunning views of Kangyatse.

Altitude 4841m
Distance 11 km
Duration 6 hours

Day 5: Nimaling to Chokdo

Ascend Kongmarula and enjoy views of Kangyatse peaks. Then descend till Chokdo and drive to Leh. This is the most challenging but rewarding day of the trek.

Pass Height 5260m
Distance 17 km
Duration 8 hours

Trek Overview

Suitable for most hill walkers and generally involving shorter days at moderate altitude, on good paths and tracks. However, a reasonable level of fitness is required as these treks can involve considerable amounts of ascent and descent and the occasional difficult day.

Acclimatization

The first day has been kept free for getting acclimatized to the low level of oxygen in Ladakh. We will do short visits to places around in a vehicle and take short walks around the town.

Monastery Visits

Thiksey Monastery

Start early to attend the morning prayers at Thiksey Monastery, a majestic 12-storey complex perched atop a hill, offering a spiritually immersive experience. Explore its prayer halls, murals, and the grand 15-meter-tall Maitreya Buddha statue.

Hemis Monastery

Located 40 km from Leh, known for its rich history, impressive artwork, and its role as the spiritual seat of the Drukpa lineage. Return to Leh in the evening.
